Invitation for Global Cryptocurrency Companies

The Pakistan Virtual Asset Regulatory Authority (PVARA) has announced a call for Expressions of Interest (EoIs) from cryptocurrency firms worldwide. This move is part of a broader effort to create a clear and accountable digital finance framework.

Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for licensing under PVARA, companies must meet specific requirements:

  • Firms must already hold approval from major global regulators, such as the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the UK Financial Conduct Authority.
  • Proposals should include comprehensive details regarding the company's profile, existing licenses, and history of compliance.

Regulatory Goals

PVARA's licensing framework is aimed at combating illicit financial activities while simultaneously encouraging the development of fintech and Shariah-compliant products. This dual approach emphasizes both compliance and innovation within the industry.

In a further push to integrate cryptocurrency into its economy, Pakistan is tapping into its surplus electricity for Bitcoin mining. However, this initiative has drawn scrutiny from the International Monetary Fund (IMF), which has raised concerns regarding the energy consumption associated with such practices.
