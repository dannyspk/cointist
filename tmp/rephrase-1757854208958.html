Licensing Invitation by PVARA

The Virtual Asset Regulatory Authority of Pakistan (PVARA) is now welcoming applications from global cryptocurrency companies keen on obtaining licenses to operate in the country. This move reflects Pakistan's intention to integrate itself into the evolving landscape of digital finance.

Eligibility Criteria for Applications

To qualify, firms must hold licenses from well-regarded global regulatory entities, such as:

  • U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
  • Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) in the UK

Applicants will be required to submit detailed documentation, including:

  • Company profiles
  • Current licenses
  • Proposed services
  • Compliance records

Aims of the New Regulatory Framework

This new licensing framework is designed not only to mitigate risks associated with illicit financial activities but also to encourage innovation in the fintech sector. Additionally, it focuses on promoting Shariah-compliant financial products, aligning with local cultural and religious values.

Pakistan's Growing Crypto Landscape

According to Chainalysis' 2025 index, Pakistan ranks third globally in cryptocurrency adoption, underscoring a significant market potential. As the demand for crypto solutions rises, the regulatory framework aims to harness this potential responsibly.

Future Initiatives in Crypto Mining

Pakistan is also laying plans to establish a Bitcoin Strategic Reserve. Furthermore, the government intends to channel surplus electricity towards cryptocurrency mining operations, enhancing the infrastructure for digital currencies.

Concerns from the IMF

However, concerns have been raised by the International Monetary Fund (IMF) regarding the high energy consumption associated with mining activities. These apprehensions may pose challenges to the future of the crypto sector in Pakistan, particularly in terms of sustainable practices.
