Invitation to International Firms

The Pakistan Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (PVARA) has issued a call for Expressions of Interest (EoIs) from global crypto companies interested in establishing a presence in Pakistan. This initiative is part of the country's broader strategy to engage with the international crypto community.

Emphasis on Compliance and Transparency

In its invitation, PVARA underscores the importance of adherence to international regulatory standards and a commitment to transparency. These factors are deemed essential for fostering a trustworthy environment in the digital asset space.

Eligibility Criteria for Firms

To qualify for consideration, interested firms must:

  • Possess licenses from renowned regulatory bodies, including the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the UK Financial Conduct Authority.
  • Provide comprehensive details about their company profiles and the services they offer.
  • Demonstrate a solid compliance record, particularly tailored to the Pakistani market.

Objectives of PVARA

PVARA's efforts are driven by dual objectives: to mitigate illicit financing activities and to promote innovations in the fintech sector. Notably, the authority is also exploring the development of Shariah-compliant financial products to cater to local preferences.

Pakistan's Position in Global Crypto Adoption

According to Chainalysis' 2025 Global Crypto Adoption Index, Pakistan is currently ranked third worldwide in terms of crypto adoption. This ranking highlights the growing interest and engagement of Pakistani citizens in digital assets.

Recent Initiatives in Crypto Sector

In addition to the licensing initiative, Pakistan has unveiled several projects aimed at enhancing its crypto landscape. These include:

  • The establishment of a Bitcoin Strategic Reserve to solidify its position in the crypto market.
  • Plans to allocate surplus electricity specifically for crypto mining operations.

These initiatives signal Pakistan's commitment to developing a robust framework for cryptocurrency operations, aiming to balance regulatory oversight with innovation in the financial sector.
