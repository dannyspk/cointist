Global Invitation via PVARA

Pakistan has officially invited international cryptocurrency firms to apply for licenses through the Pakistan Virtual Asset Regulatory Authority (PVARA). This initiative marks a significant step in establishing a regulated framework for digital assets in the country.

Eligibility Criteria for Firms

To qualify for licensing, firms must meet specific eligibility criteria, which include:

  • Recognition from prominent global financial regulators.
  • Prior experience in cryptocurrency operations and technology.

Submission Guidelines for Expressions of Interest

PVARA has outlined the requirements for submitting Expressions of Interest (EoIs). Interested firms need to provide detailed documentation demonstrating their compliance with local regulations and operational capabilities.

PVARA's Role in Regulation

The Pakistan Virtual Asset Regulatory Authority will oversee the licensing and regulation of Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) under the forthcoming Virtual Assets Ordinance 2025. This move is aimed at ensuring a secure and transparent environment for cryptocurrency transactions.

Pakistan's Position in Crypto Adoption

Recently, Pakistan has surged to third place globally in terms of cryptocurrency adoption. This rapid ascent highlights the growing interest and integration of digital assets within the country's financial system.

Statements from Bilal Bin Saqib

Bilal Bin Saqib, a prominent figure in the cryptocurrency sector, emphasized the importance of fostering a digital financial future. He advocates for the strategic development of the cryptocurrency industry in Pakistan to bolster economic growth.

Recent Developments in Bitcoin Mining and IMF Concerns

In addition to licensing efforts, Pakistan is revising its Bitcoin mining strategy. However, these initiatives are facing scrutiny amid concerns raised by the International Monetary Fund (IMF) regarding compliance and regulation within the crypto space.
