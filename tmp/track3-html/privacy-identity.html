Privacy & Identity

Intermediate 10–14 min read

DEFINITION

Privacy preserves selective disclosure; identity binds attestations to users. Together they enable compliant, user‑controlled access and reputation.

Privacy tools

Zero-knowledge proofs, stealth addresses, and mixers-with-guardrails. Weigh usability vs compliance friction.

Identity layers

Verifiable credentials, ENS-style naming, and soulbound/bound tokens for reputation and access control.
